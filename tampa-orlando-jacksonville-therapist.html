Beyond Miami

Different Cities, Similar Weight.

Most of Florida's executive therapy options are concentrated in Miami or not specialized at all. Tampa, Orlando, and Jacksonville each have their own industries, their own cultures, and their own version of leadership pressure. A generalist in any of those cities will understand your zip code but not your board meeting.

Tampa runs on healthcare, financial services, and a growing tech presence. The executives here carry the weight of industries where mistakes have real consequences and regulatory oversight is constant. Raymond James, BayCare, the insurance corridor along I-4. The leadership culture tends to be quieter than Miami, more operational than flashy. The strain shows up differently: less performance anxiety, more accumulated fatigue from years of steady, high-responsibility work.

Orlando's executive population splits between the tourism and hospitality giants and a newer wave of tech, simulation, and defense companies. Leading in a city most people associate with vacations creates a strange dissonance. The work is serious. The external perception is not. For founders building in Orlando's growing tech corridor, there is the added challenge of doing it outside the obvious markets, which means fewer peers and fewer people who understand what the work costs. For more on what that isolation does over time, see the loneliness of leadership.

Jacksonville is one of the largest cities in the country by area and one of the least visible in the founder conversation. Financial services, logistics, healthcare, and a large military-adjacent community. The executives here are often running operations at real scale without the cultural permission to talk about what that costs them personally. The Navy presence shapes the leadership culture in ways that go unacknowledged: stoicism, self-reliance, discomfort with asking for help.

Who I Work With

Leaders Across Florida's Major Markets.

One Florida license covers the entire state. The work is the same whether you are in Tampa, Orlando, Jacksonville, or anywhere else.

Healthcare and Finance Executives (Tampa)

Leaders in hospital systems, insurance, and financial services carrying the specific strain of industries where the margin for error is thin and the regulatory burden is constant.

Tech and Simulation Leaders (Orlando)

Founders and executives in Orlando's defense simulation, AR/VR, and growing SaaS community. Building in a market that does not get the same attention as the coasts, which creates its own set of pressures.

Corporate and Financial Services Leaders (Jacksonville)

Senior leaders at Jacksonville's large financial institutions, logistics companies, and healthcare networks. Operational scale with less cultural acknowledgment of the psychological cost.

Founders Outside the Major Metros

Building something from Gainesville, Fort Lauderdale, St. Petersburg, Sarasota, Naples, or anywhere else in Florida. Telehealth means the same access regardless of location.

Dual-Career Couples

When both partners are in demanding careers and the relationship absorbs the overflow. Couples therapy for the specific friction that high-stakes work puts on a partnership. More on couples therapy for founders.

Military-Adjacent and Defense Executives

Leaders in defense contracting, military-connected industries, and government-adjacent roles. A culture that rewards composure and rarely makes room for the personal cost of leadership.

Why Telehealth

Same Therapist, Wherever You Are in Florida.

Specialized therapy for executives is hard to find in any city. In Tampa, Orlando, and Jacksonville it is particularly thin. Telehealth solves that. You get a therapist who works exclusively with founders and executives, without being limited to whoever happens to practice in your metro.

Sessions happen from wherever you have privacy and a screen. No commute, no waiting room, no shared parking lot with someone on your board. Early morning and evening slots available. About Diana

Who You'd Be Working With.

Diana Chu is a Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist in Florida (TPMF785) and California (LMFT #105546), and a Registered Drama Therapist (RDT #659). She holds a Master of Arts in Counseling Psychology from the California Institute of Integral Studies and serves as an Interpersonal Dynamics Facilitator at Stanford University Graduate School of Business.

The practice is built around the psychology of high performance. That means the work starts from an understanding of what leadership actually costs, not a generic intake process that spends weeks catching up. If you are running a company, managing a portfolio, or leading an organization, the first session is already about your situation, not about explaining your job.
