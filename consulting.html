01

Clinical Supervision Toward Licensure

Individual supervision for AMFTs, APCCs, and ASWs working toward licensure in California. Diana is a BBS-approved supervisor with a practice rooted in relational, somatic, and experiential approaches. Supervision is a space to think carefully about your clinical work, develop your therapeutic identity, and build confidence as a practitioner.

BBS-Approved Supervision

Hours count toward AMFT, APCC, and ASW licensure requirements. Compliant with California BBS standards.

Relational and Experiential Focus

Supervision informed by Diana's training in drama therapy, somatic approaches, and interpersonal dynamics. Not just case review.

Support for Complex Cases

Executives, founders, high-achieving clients, trauma, and couples work. Specialty population experience available.

Professional Development

Guidance on building a private practice, niche development, and career direction alongside clinical skill building.

02

Consultation Groups for Clinicians

A small, ongoing consultation group for licensed therapists who want to think more rigorously about their clinical work. Diana has facilitated groups for clinicians working with high-achieving clients and is opening space for a new cohort. Groups are kept intentionally small to allow for real depth and trust to develop over time.

Case Consultation

Bring your stuck cases, countertransference, and clinical questions to a facilitated group that takes the work seriously.

Peer Community

Build lasting collegial relationships with other thoughtful clinicians. Private practice can be isolating. This is a corrective for that.

Specialty Focus

Groups are shaped around shared clinical interests. Current focus: high-achieving clients, executives, founders, and couples.

New Cohort Forming

Spots in the next group are limited. Reach out now to learn more and be considered for an upcoming opening.

03

Corporate Wellness for Tech Companies

Diana partners with tech companies, People teams, and ERG groups to bring clinical expertise directly into the workplace. Drawing on her work as an Interpersonal Dynamics Facilitator at Stanford GSB and her experience with executives and founders, she brings a credible, grounded perspective on what high-performing teams actually need to sustain themselves.

Burnout Prevention

Workshops and speaking engagements on recognizing, addressing, and preventing burnout in high-demand environments.

Team Resilience

Programming designed to help teams communicate under pressure, process setbacks, and sustain performance over time.

ERG and Affinity Group Support

Facilitated conversations and mental health programming for employee resource groups. Thoughtful, clinically grounded, not generic wellness content.

Leadership Mental Health

Targeted support for managers and leaders navigating the psychological weight of leading people through uncertainty and growth.
