Service 01

Individual Therapy for Executives & Founders

High performance demands come with a particular kind of psychological weight, one that most people in your orbit aren't equipped to understand, let alone help you carry.

Imposter syndrome. Decision fatigue. The blurring of identity with role. The loneliness of leadership. The fear that if you slow down, everything falls apart. These aren't signs of weakness. They're occupational hazards, and they're treatable.

In individual therapy, we work to untangle the patterns that keep you stuck, develop genuine psychological resilience (not just better coping mechanisms), and build the kind of inner clarity that makes you a better leader and a more present person.

Diana draws on EMDR, somatic approaches, and evidence-based methods tailored to how executives actually think and process. No generic worksheets. No homework that doesn't fit your life. The work is specific to you.

Leadership Transitions

Navigating identity shifts, new roles, and the weight of growing responsibility.

Burnout Recovery

Diagnosing and reversing the specific patterns of high-performance burnout.

Executive Anxiety

Anxiety, perfectionism, and decision fatigue addressed at their root, not just managed.

Founder Identity

Processing exits, liquidity events, and the loss of the company as identity anchor.

Service 02

Couples Therapy for High-Achieving Partners

Founding a company changes you. It changes your relationship too, often in ways that are hard to name until you are already in the middle of it. Whether you and your partner are both founders or one of you carries that world while the other holds everything else together, the pressures are real and they are specific.

For co-founding couples, the boundary between business partnership and intimate partnership blurs constantly. Decisions about the company become decisions about the relationship. Disagreements at work follow you home. The roles you play during the day do not disappear when the laptop closes.

For couples where one partner is a founder, the asymmetry creates its own strain. One person is consumed by a mission. The other absorbs the emotional overflow. Both end up feeling unseen. The founder carries the weight of the company. Their partner carries the weight of the founder.

This work is not about assigning blame or relitigating old arguments. It is about building a shared language for what you both actually need and creating a relationship that can hold the full complexity of the lives you are building. Gottman Method informed and adapted for how high-achieving couples actually operate.

Co-Founding Dynamics

Navigating the uncommon tension of being business partners and life partners at the same time, and learning where one role ends and the other begins.

When One Partner Is the Founder

Addressing the asymmetry of one partner being all-consumed and the other carrying the emotional weight of that, so neither person feels invisible.

Communication Under Pressure

Breaking the cycles of conflict, withdrawal, and resentment that build when both people are running on empty.

Intimacy & Reconnection

Rebuilding closeness after a fundraise, an exit, a hard season, or simply years of putting the relationship last.
